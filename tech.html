Technical architecture

Closed-loop systems explained for technical buyers.

This page breaks down how AutoCDP turns dealership data into per-recipient opportunity scoring, compliance-aware execution, direct-mail orchestration, and Learning Ledger feedback. It is written to explain the operating system behind the product, not just the marketing layer.

Closed-loop system

What closed loop means here

Closed loop means the system does more than send campaigns. It identifies an opportunity, chooses a channel, launches the execution, records the downstream response, and then uses that result to improve the next decision. That is why the Learning Ledger matters: it keeps the platform from repeating weak creative or weak sequencing.

Opportunity Engine

Per-recipient decisioning

The Opportunity Engine evaluates each customer individually instead of depending on blunt list pulls. That lets the platform decide whether the customer belongs in service recovery, reactivation, loyalty, trade, or another motion before any message is sent.

Compliance spine

Compliance by construction

The system is described as governed by a structural Compliance & Learning Spine. In practice, that means pre-flight gates, generation-time controls, and operating constraints are part of the flow itself rather than last-minute review steps bolted on afterward.

Execution graph

How channels fit together

SMS, email, and direct mail are coordinated as one operating graph. The point is not to run channels independently; it is to choose the right mix, measure the response, and write outcomes back into the next cycle of decisioning.

System flow

Connect → Score → Generate → Gate → Execute → Learn

Connect dealership systems without forcing a rip-and-replace of the existing stack.
Score opportunity per recipient through the Opportunity Engine.
Generate channel-ready campaigns through the agentic execution layer.
Apply compliance and governance rules before launch.
Execute through digital and physical channels.
Record response into the Learning Ledger and use it to improve the next campaign.
Why this matters

From architecture to operations

Most dealerships already have data. The technical problem is turning fragmented records into governed execution and measurable learning. AutoCDP’s closed-loop framing is meant to solve exactly that gap: not just visibility, but action plus feedback.
